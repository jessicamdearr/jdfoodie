This weekend was the perfect excuse for a food scoot! Food and drinks are my favorite way to have a good time. I started off at Wasa, a great sushi restaurant at the Spectrum, and tried some of their specialty tasters, which included nigiri style sushi pieces with assorted fish and garnishes. Front and center was their seared salmon topped with colored daikon and green onions.

Next up was a visit to the Anaheim Packing House. I absolutely love the interior decor of this place. There is a perfect mix of straight lines and softness where industrial and floral feels work together. There are skylights lining the roof and everything is so lively and bright. Live bands perform during the day, making it even more happy of a place. It's great to get together with friends here and relax over some awesome food and drinks.

There are so many different restaurants at the packing house that it is hard to know where to start! I was able to try a pretty good variety of street food here, including banh mi sandwiches and spring rolls from Sawleaf Cafe, tri tip from Georgia's and fish & chips from Chippy. The banh mi was probably my favorite, though everything was so good! The Hammer bar has really amazing mixologists, one of which being some guy with a cool mustache who gave me a wonderful replacement of a spicy Bloody Mary.

The absolute BEST part of the Anaheim Packing House is the secret speakeasy called the Blind Rabbit. Everything about it is perfect, down to the entrance and exit. The inside is dimmed and cozy, and the bartenders in here are amazing as well. My friends and I had a few rounds of all kinds of different drinks, none of which were anything less than great.
